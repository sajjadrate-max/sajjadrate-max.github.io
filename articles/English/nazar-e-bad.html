What Is the Evil Eye and How to Guard Against It

The concept of "the evil eye" (nazar-e-bad) is mentioned in the Hadith as well. According to Islamic teaching, another person's envious gaze can cause harm, which is why protective supplications have been taught.

Traditional Signs

Sudden unease without apparent cause, lethargy, unexplained worry, or obstacles in one's work are sometimes traditionally associated with the evil eye. However, it is not necessarily the case that every difficulty stems from this cause.

Recommended Methods of Protection

Reciting Al-Mu'awwidhatayn (Surah Al-Falaq and Surah An-Nas), Ayat al-Kursi, and the recommended supplications are advised for protection against the evil eye. Likewise, saying "MashaAllah" and "BarakAllah" upon seeing something good is also considered a means of protection against it.

A Word of Caution

If persistent, unusual distress is felt, one should, alongside spiritual supplications, also consult a qualified physician, since many symptoms can have physical or psychological causes as well.
