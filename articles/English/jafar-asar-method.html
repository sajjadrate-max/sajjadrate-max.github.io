The Method of Ilm-e-Jafar Asar

Ilm-e-Jafar Asar is the second major branch of Ilm-e-Jafar, based mostly on numerical calculation. This method considers four elements: the questioner's name, the mother's name, the day the question is asked, and the letters of the question itself.

The Calculation Method

The Abjad values of these four elements are extracted and added together. The resulting sum is then divided by 4. The remainder left after this division — that is, 0, 1, 2, or 3 — determines the nature of the outcome.

Traditional Meanings of the Remainders

Traditionally, each remainder points toward a particular kind of underlying cause — for instance, one number may point toward a physical cause, another toward a spiritual disturbance, and another toward a merely temporary difficulty. These interpretations are recorded with slight variations across different traditional texts.

Keep in Mind

Ilm-e-Jafar Asar, too, is simply a traditional means of guidance. For serious medical, psychological, or other critical matters, consulting the relevant professionals is essential, and for spiritual matters, it is best to consult a pious and trustworthy scholar.
