The Method and Importance of Istikhara Prayer

Istikhara prayer is an important and recommended (Sunnah) practice in Islam, performed to seek Allah's guidance toward what is best before making any significant decision.

How to Perform Istikhara

Typically, two units (rak'ahs) of voluntary prayer are offered, in which reciting Surah Al-Kafirun in the first unit and Surah Al-Ikhlas in the second is considered commendable. After completing the prayer, the well-known supplication of Istikhara is recited, asking Allah that if the matter is good, He make it easy, and if not, He turn it away.

Waiting for the Outcome

After performing Istikhara, it is not necessary that the answer come through a dream. Often, a sense of ease in the heart or circumstances becoming favorable is itself a form of answer. Patience and trust in Allah are an essential part of this practice.

Summary

Istikhara prayer is an excellent, recommended practice before any important decision. It can also be combined with any traditional method (including Ilm-e-Jafar), but ultimate trust should always rest with Allah alone.
